Materials R&D

We apply machine learning algorithms to Materials R&D, helping shorten the materials development cycle. By using data from ab-initio simulations to supplement existing experimental data we enable clients to explore chemical design space more quickly.

Our first pilot focused on the corrosion resistance of steel. With a one week turnaround, our algorithms were able to predict the properties with over 90% accuracy, greatly reducing the number of experiments that would need to be run to develop novel steel grades.

Materials Selection

Our machine learning algorithms can help in the selection of the most appropriate material given the customer's requirements. Our algorithms work in industries from Construction and Oil & Gas to Automotive and Aerospace & Defence.

For example, the construction industry currently relies on crude rule-based systems for estimating corrosion and selecting materials. Our methodology provides highly granular predictive models that can discover instances where traditional methods result in an overengineering of the steel grades used in a given application.
