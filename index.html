High Speed Materials Development

We enable rapid, cost-effective materials development through use of cutting-edge algorithms and large datasets.

Technology

We use machine learning algorithms, which are able to extract patterns from highly nonlinear, correlated and noisy data, to predict the properties of materials before they are even synthesised. This enables a much more efficient targeting of experiments, and a concomitant decrease in the time and cost required to obtain a viable new material product.

In addition to experimental data, we train our models on large sets of computational chemistry simulations. This additional data source provides a way to extrapolate the experimental data into new regions of chemical design space.

Applications

While our approach is in principle applicable across a range of domains, our initial focus is on developing novel compounds for organic light-emitting diodes (OLEDs), a technology that is being used in display applications where it provides improved contrast and reduced device complexity compared to conventional LCD technology.
